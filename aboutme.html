About Me

In March 2023, Kay will graduate from Portland State University with a Bachelors of Science in Computer Science. Though she was introduced to computer coding through artistic endeavours, she has an interest in cyber security and hopes to get an internship in that field during her last term at Portland State University.

Her coding roots started with her collaborative gallery exhibits back when she was pursuing her Associates of Science in Music and Sonic Arts at Portland Community College. During this time, she discovered how to create digital interactive art. Along with classmates and friends she met along the way, Kay became an artist in a few gallery exhibits before COVID-19. She created art through coding (mainly in Java). By using the Microsoft Kinect, Processing, Java, JavaScript, Arduino, Microcontrollers, and more, she created art for the public to interact with that blurred the lines between technology and art. The juxtaposition of the strict logical components of computers and the lucid emotional passion of art fueled her inspiration.

Kay has always enjoyed math and art. This webside showcases some projects that she did for another class at Portland State University, "Exploring Fractals". This class was a perfect combination of art and mathematics. She highly recommends it for all students interested in math and coding, and using it to discover and create art.
